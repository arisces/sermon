The Secret Life That Sets the Whole Church on Fire

A Sermon Based on the Teachings of Charles G. Finney

Main Text: Matthew 6:6 (NIV)

Big Idea: When believers cultivate a hidden life of surrendered, prevailing prayer in the secret place, the Holy Spirit ignites them with a fire that sets entire churches—and communities—ablaze for the Kingdom of God.

Introduction: The Crisis of Powerless Christianity

There is a secret that transformed ordinary men into spiritual giants, a secret that caused entire churches to be shaken by the power of God. This secret has been buried under religious programs, gospel entertainment, and lukewarm Christianity.

Why are so many churches full of activity yet empty of power? Why do multitudes attend, but few truly intercede? Why does religious commotion replace genuine encounter? The answer is not found in better marketing strategies or more relevant music. It is not discovered in the latest church growth methodologies.

God is not looking for extraordinary talents, education, or eloquence. He is looking for completely surrendered hearts where Christ can live unhindered.

The prophet Samuel understood this when he declared, "Does the Lord delight in burnt offerings and sacrifices as much as in obeying the Lord? To obey is better than sacrifice, and to heed is better than the fat of rams" (1 Samuel 15:22, NIV).

A revival may be expected when Christians have a spirit of prayer for a revival—that is, when they pray as if their hearts were set upon it. When they go about groaning out their heart's desire. When they have real travail of soul.— Charles G. Finney

The question before God's people today is this: What is the kind of prayer that God uses to set a church on fire with revival, and what does it cost to obtain it?

• • •

I. The Secret Place: Where Fire Is Kindled in Secret

Jesus taught His disciples, "But when you pray, go into your room, close the door and pray to your Father, who is unseen. Then your Father, who sees what is done in secret, will reward you" (Matthew 6:6, NIV).

The fire of revival does not fall on churches with the best programs or most talented leaders. It falls on churches whose people have learned the secret of prevailing prayer in hidden places.

Fire always begins in hidden places before manifesting publicly. The secret place creates the public manifestation. Before the fire descends publicly, someone is burning secretly. Before multitudes are reached, an intercessor is in agony. Before harvest appears, someone sows with tears in hidden dependence.

The psalmist declared, "He who dwells in the shelter of the Most High will rest in the shadow of the Almighty. I will say of the Lord, 'He is my refuge and my fortress, my God, in whom I trust'" (Psalm 91:1-2, NIV). This is the secret place—not a physical location, but a position of the heart.

Finney's Practice

"I was accustomed to use my voice in private prayer. For convenience sake, that I might not be heard, I had spread a buffalo robe on the hayloft, where I used to spend much of my time in secret prayer to God. I was in the habit of praying every Saturday night till after midnight for the descent of the Holy Ghost among them."

When Finney was converted, it was in a grove where he went to pray. Very soon after his conversion, several other cases of conversion occurred that were reported to have taken place under similar circumstances—persons went up into the grove to pray, and there made their peace with God.

The secret place is not reserved for preachers. It is available to every son and daughter of God. The mother praying for her children in the early morning hours. The student seeking God in their dorm room. The businessman who transforms his office into an altar. The father who intercedes for his family. Ordinary people willing to create space for the extraordinary.

As Jesus promised, "Ask and it will be given to you; seek and you will find; knock and the door will be opened to you. For everyone who asks receives; the one who seeks finds; and to the one who knocks, the door will be opened" (Matthew 7:7-8, NIV).

• • •

II. The Spirit of True Prayer: More Than Words

What constitutes the spirit of prayer? Is it saying many prayers with warm words? Is it following some religious ritual or routine?

Prevailing prayer is that which secures an answer. Saying prayers is not offering prevailing prayer. The prevalence of prayer does not depend so much on quantity as on quality. Effective prayer is prayer that attains what it seeks. It is prayer that moves God, effecting its end.— Charles G. Finney

The spirit of prayer, according to Finney, "is a state of continual desire and anxiety of mind for the salvation of sinners. It is something that weighs down the heart of the believer. It is the subject of their thoughts all the time."

This is not enthusiasm. It is exactly what the apostle Paul felt when he said, "My little children, of whom I travail in birth." This travail of soul is that deep agony which people feel when they lay hold on God for a blessing and will not let Him go until they receive it.

James wrote, "The prayer of a righteous person is powerful and effective. Elijah was a human being, even as we are. He prayed earnestly that it would not rain, and it did not rain on the land for three and a half years. Again he prayed, and the heavens gave rain, and the earth produced its crops" (James 5:16-18, NIV).

Finney's Testimony

"I have known persons pray till they were all wet with perspiration, in the coldest weather in winter. I have known persons pray for hours, till their strength was all exhausted with the agony of their minds. Such prayers prevailed with God."

The apostle Paul understood this when he wrote, "In the same way, the Spirit helps us in our weakness. We do not know what we ought to pray for, but the Spirit himself intercedes for us through wordless groans" (Romans 8:26, NIV).

Jesus Himself taught us to pray with persistence: "Suppose one of you has a friend, and he goes to him at midnight and says, 'Friend, lend me three loaves of bread, because a friend of mine on a journey has come to me, and I have nothing to set before him.' Then the one inside answers, 'Don't bother me. The door is already locked, and my children are with me in bed. I can't get up and give you anything.' I tell you, though he will not get up and give him the bread because he is his friend, yet because of the man's boldness he will get up and give him as much as he needs" (Luke 11:5-8, NIV).

Prayer becomes powerful not through method but through surrender. When prayers cease to be negotiations and become worship. When the heart stops demanding and begins to yield.

• • •

III. The Price of Fire: Death to Self and Total Surrender

Here is the hinge upon which everything turns. Prayer becomes powerful when the vessel is emptied of self and broken sufficiently for God to fill completely.

A willingness to deny self is the very spirit of Christ. It is the heart and soul of the Gospel. When you come back to God for pardon and salvation, come with all you have to lay all at His feet. Give up all that you are and all that you have to God, and take Him at His word. This is the only way to find peace and rest for your souls.— Charles G. Finney

The cost is everything. Your agenda, ambition, pride, reputation, comfort, security—everything you think you cannot live without. God does not share His throne. He wants complete control, not second place.

Jesus said, "Whoever wants to be my disciple must deny themselves and take up their cross daily and follow me. For whoever wants to save their life will lose it, but whoever loses their life for me will save it" (Luke 9:23-24, NIV).

Paul captured this mystery when he wrote, "I have been crucified with Christ and I no longer live, but Christ lives in me. The life I now live in the body, I live by faith in the Son of God, who loved me and gave himself for me" (Galatians 2:20, NIV).

This is a daily crucifixion, constant death to self, absolute submission. The kingdom of heaven suffers violence, and the violent take it by force. As Finney explained, you must do violence to your own will, your own plans, your own ego until nothing remains but Christ.

John the Baptist understood this when he declared, "He must become greater; I must become less" (John 3:30, NIV).

God cannot trust His power to vessels still full of themselves. Finney often spoke of his own need for brokenness and surrender. He wrote about being in a state of great dissatisfaction with his own want of stability in faith and love. He needed frequently to hold days of fasting and prayer, and to spend much time in overhauling his own religious life, in order to retain that communion with God.

The Key

God does not anoint abilities—He anoints brokenness. He does not use talent—He uses surrender. He does not seek strength—He seeks recognized weakness so His power can be manifested.

Paul understood this when he wrote, "But he said to me, 'My grace is sufficient for you, for my power is made perfect in weakness.' Therefore I will boast all the more gladly about my weaknesses, so that Christ's power may rest on me. That is why, for Christ's sake, I delight in weaknesses, in insults, in hardships, in persecutions, in difficulties. For when I am weak, then I am strong" (2 Corinthians 12:9-10, NIV).

• • •

IV. The Upper Room Pattern: Waiting Until Fire Falls

The apostles had walked with Jesus, witnessed miracles and resurrection. Yet they still lacked power—they had to wait in the upper room. The Scripture records, "They all joined together constantly in prayer, along with the women and Mary the mother of Jesus, and with his brothers" (Acts 1:14, NIV).

They remained in prayer, unity, and complete dependence until fire descended. When the day of Pentecost came, they were all together in one place. Suddenly a sound like the blowing of a violent wind came from heaven and filled the whole house where they were sitting. They saw what seemed to be tongues of fire that separated and came to rest on each of them. All of them were filled with the Holy Spirit (Acts 2:1-4, NIV).

That fire did not illuminate just one room—it set an entire city ablaze. And from that city, the fire spread to other cities, other nations, until the entire world was reached.

The apostles transformed the world not through eloquence or program, but through days of hidden prayer in the upper room until they became empty vessels the Holy Spirit could fill.

Peter, who had denied Jesus three times, stood up on the day of Pentecost and proclaimed, "Repent and be baptized, every one of you, in the name of Jesus Christ for the forgiveness of your sins. And you will receive the gift of the Holy Spirit. The promise is for you and your children and for all who are far off—for all whom the Lord our God will call" (Acts 2:38-39, NIV). That day, about three thousand were added to their number. This is the power that comes from waiting in the secret place until fire falls.

Many want the mantle without the brokenness, want the authority without the humiliation, want to operate in power without going through the process. But God does not work that way.

Everyone wants the glory of Pentecost, but few want to wait ten days in the upper room. And because of this, we have churches full of activity but empty of power. We have services full of noise but empty of presence.

• • •

V. The Man of Prayer: Father Nash

Finney knew a man named Father Nash who was most wonderful in prayer—one of the most earnest, devout, spiritually-minded, heavenly-minded men Finney ever saw. He gave himself up to almost constant prayer, literally praying himself to death at last.

He had a praying list of persons whom he made subjects of prayer every day, and sometimes many times a day. When Finney got to town to start a revival, a boarding house lady said, "Brother Finney, do you know a Father Nash? He and two other men have been at my boarding house for the last three days, but they haven't eaten a bite of food. I opened the door and peeped in at them because I could hear them groaning, and I saw them down on their faces."

When the public meetings started, Father Nash would not usually attend but kept praying in his closet for the convicting power of the Holy Spirit to fall on the crowd and melt their hearts. When opposition arose, Father Nash would pray all the harder.

This is the kind of hidden intercession that prepares the way for revival. Not public performance, but secret agony. Not platform presence, but closet persistence.

The apostle Paul instructed the church: "And pray in the Spirit on all occasions with all kinds of prayers and requests. With this in mind, be alert and always keep on praying for all the Lord's people" (Ephesians 6:18, NIV).

• • •

VI. The Call to Decision: Will You Pay the Price?

This may be your hour—God has orchestrated circumstances, timing, everything—so you would hear this message now. Do not let it pass.

Will you do it? Have you gone over with your sins and confessed them and gotten them all out of the way? Can you pray now? Will you join and offer prevailing prayer that the Spirit of God may come down here?— Charles G. Finney

The prophet Isaiah issued a similar call: "Seek the Lord while he may be found; call on him while he is near. Let the wicked forsake their ways and the unrighteous their thoughts. Let them turn to the Lord, and he will have mercy on them, and to our God, for he will freely pardon" (Isaiah 55:6-7, NIV).

Stop playing at Christianity. Stop pretending everything is fine when you know it is not. Stop settling for crumbs when God offers His presence. Stop living on the periphery when God is calling you to the center of His will.

Stop trying to live for God and simply allow God to live through you.

Do not wait for better conditions—surrender is a present, immediate yielding. The fire spreads when one person burns completely for God. Be that one person who pays the price others refuse to pay.

Finney's Warning

"Unless you have the spirit of prayer, you can do nothing. You may preach, you may talk, you may distribute literature, you may do many things, but without prayer, without the Spirit of God, you will do nothing for the salvation of souls."

The author of Hebrews exhorts us: "Let us then approach God's throne of grace with confidence, so that we may receive mercy and find grace to help us in our time of need" (Hebrews 4:16, NIV).

• • •

Conclusion: It Always Begins With One

It begins with one surrendered life—one person willing to enter the process of brokenness and total surrender.

Noah alone preaching righteousness for 120 years. Abraham alone leaving everything to follow God's call. Elijah alone confronting Baal on Mount Carmel. John the Baptist alone crying in the wilderness. Jesus alone carrying the cross. The apostles few in number waiting in the upper room.

It always begins with one.

Without total surrender, you can preach, sing, serve—but produce no eternal fruit. With total surrender, dormant resurrection power awakens within you. What begins as one burning life spreads to families, communities, churches, cities.

The fire of revival does not fall on churches with the best programs or most talented leaders. It falls on churches whose people have learned the secret of total surrender and prevailing prayer.

Paul wrote to the Philippians: "Continue to work out your salvation with fear and trembling, for it is God who works in you to will and to act in order to fulfill his good purpose" (Philippians 2:12-13, NIV).

If you would have this spirit of prayer, you must give yourselves to prayer. You must make time for prayer. You must pray in your families. You must pray in secret. You must pray much. You must pray earnestly. You must pray in faith. You must pray with perseverance.

May God help us to be a praying people, and to seek His face until He comes and rains righteousness upon us.

Altar Call Prayer

"Here I am—take everything—break me, empty me, ignite in me a fire that will never go out."

What differentiates ordinary Christianity from life that sets multitudes ablaze is not gift, talent, education, or opportunity. It is total surrender—a life hidden with Christ in God, completely yielded to His will and His glory.

Amen.

Post-Sermon Application Points

For Believers

Create your "upper room"—a specific place and time for secret prayer daily
Develop a praying list of people to intercede for regularly
Cry out for brokenness: "Lord, break me, empty me, consume me"
Face your "Ishmaels"—every attempt to do God's work in your own strength
Join with others for corporate prayer focused on revival

For Those Who Do Not Yet Know Christ

Come to Him today—don't wait for perfect conditions
Lay down your life at His feet completely
Give up all you are and have to God without reservation
Take Him at His word—this is the only way to true peace

For All of Us

Make the decision of total surrender right now, today
Recognize you are at the breaking point—tired of lifeless religion
Stop negotiating with God and simply say "yes" without conditions
Ask God to give us the spirit of prayer
Ask Him to break our hearts for the lost
Ask Him to make us willing to pay the price of revival
