The Secret Life That Sets the Whole Church on Fire

A Sermon Based on the Teachings of Charles G. Finney

Main Text: Matthew 6:6 (NIV)

Big Idea: When believers cultivate a hidden life of surrendered, prevailing prayer in the secret place, the Holy Spirit ignites them with a fire that sets entire churches—and communities—ablaze for the Kingdom of God.

INTRODUCTION: The Crisis of Powerless Christianity

There is a secret that transformed ordinary men into spiritual giants, a secret that caused entire churches to be shaken by the power of God. This secret has been buried under religious programs, gospel entertainment, and lukewarm Christianity.

Why are so many churches full of activity yet empty of power? Why do multitudes attend, but few truly intercede? Why does religious commotion replace genuine encounter? The answer is not found in better marketing strategies or more relevant music. It is not discovered in the latest church growth methodologies.

God is not looking for extraordinary talents, education, or eloquence. He is looking for completely surrendered hearts where Christ can live unhindered.

Scripture Reference

"Does the Lord delight in burnt offerings and sacrifices as much as in obeying the Lord? To obey is better than sacrifice, and to heed is better than the fat of rams." (1 Samuel 15:22, NIV)

Key Quote from Finney

A revival may be expected when Christians have a spirit of prayer for a revival—that is, when they pray as if their hearts were set upon it. When they go about groaning out their heart's desire. When they have real travail of soul.— Charles G. Finney

The question before God's people today is this: What is the kind of prayer that God uses to set a church on fire with revival, and what does it cost to obtain it?

1 THE SECRET PLACE: Where Fire Is Kindled in Secret

Scripture Teaching

"But when you pray, go into your room, close the door and pray to your Father, who is unseen. Then your Father, who sees what is done in secret, will reward you." (Matthew 6:6, NIV)

The fire of revival does not fall on churches with the best programs or most talented leaders. It falls on churches whose people have learned the secret of prevailing prayer in hidden places.

Fire always begins in hidden places before manifesting publicly. The secret place creates the public manifestation. Before the fire descends publicly, someone is burning secretly. Before multitudes are reached, an intercessor is in agony. Before harvest appears, someone sows with tears in hidden dependence.

Core Truth: God Ignites Hearts, Not Platforms

God does not ignite platforms first. He ignites hearts.

Public fire is never the starting point; it is the result.

Matthew 6:4 – The Father who sees you will reward you openly.

What the Secret Place Creates

  • Motives are purified
  • Character is formed
  • Authority is imparted
  • Intimacy is cultivated

The secret place creates the public manifestation. Public ministry without a secret life produces noise without power.

Jesus as the Model

Jesus lived this pattern:

  • He often withdrew to lonely places to pray (Luke 5:16)
  • What He released publicly was first received privately in hidden places

Before the fire descends publicly, someone is burning secretly.

Personal Consecration in the Secret Place

Personal consecration requires:

  • No to flesh
  • Yes to obedience
  • Yes to surrender when no one is watching

Before harvest appears, someone sows with tears in a hidden place.

Psalm 126:5 states: "Those who sow in tears shall reap with joy."

Hidden obedience always precedes visible fruit. God entrusts public fire only to those who have learned to burn for Him in private.

The Pattern of Divine Revival

  • Revival is not sudden—it is revealed
  • Power is not manufactured—it is cultivated
  • Impact is not chased—it is released

Hidden fire → public glory
Secret surrender → open power

Scripture Reference

"He who dwells in the shelter of the Most High will rest in the shadow of the Almighty. I will say of the Lord, 'He is my refuge and my fortress, my God, in whom I trust.'" (Psalm 91:1-2, NIV)

This is the secret place—not a physical location, but a position of the heart.

Finney's Practice

Finney's Testimony

"I was accustomed to use my voice in private prayer. For convenience sake, that I might not be heard, I had spread a buffalo robe on the hayloft, where I used to spend much of my time in secret prayer to God. I was in the habit of praying every Saturday night till after midnight for the descent of the Holy Ghost among them."

When Finney was converted, it was in a grove where he went to pray. Very soon after his conversion, several cases of conversion occurred that were reported to have taken place under similar circumstances—persons went into the grove to pray and made their peace with God there.

The Secret Place Is Available to All

The secret place is not reserved for preachers. It is available to every son and daughter of God:

  • The mother praying for her children in the early morning hours
  • The student seeking God in their dorm room
  • The businessman who transforms his office into an altar
  • The father who intercedes for his family
  • Ordinary people willing to create space for the extraordinary

Promise of Jesus

"Ask and it will be given to you; seek and you will find; knock and the door will be opened to you. For everyone who asks receives; the one who seeks finds; and to the one who knocks, the door will be opened." (Matthew 7:7-8, NIV)

2 THE SPIRIT OF TRUE PRAYER: More Than Words

What constitutes the spirit of prayer? Is it saying many prayers with warm words? Is it following some religious ritual or routine?

Key Quote: What Is Prevailing Prayer?

Prevailing prayer is that which secures an answer. Saying prayers is not offering prevailing prayer. The prevalence of prayer does not depend so much on quantity as on quality. Effective prayer is prayer that attains what it seeks. It is prayer that moves God, effecting its end.— Charles G. Finney

Finney's Definition of the Spirit of Prayer

According to Finney, the spirit of prayer "is a state of continual desire and anxiety of mind for the salvation of sinners. It is something that weighs down the heart of the believer. It is the subject of their thoughts all the time."

This is not enthusiasm. It is exactly what the apostle Paul felt when he said, "My little children, of whom I travail in birth." This travail of soul is that deep agony which people feel when they lay hold on God for a blessing and will not let Him go until they receive it.

Scripture Reference

"The prayer of a righteous person is powerful and effective. Elijah was a human being, even as we are. He prayed earnestly that it would not rain, and it did not rain on the land for three and a half years. Again he prayed, and the heavens gave rain, and the earth produced its crops." (James 5:16-18, NIV)

Finney's Testimony on Prayer

Finney's Testimony

"I have known persons pray till they were all wet with perspiration, in the coldest weather in winter. I have known persons pray for hours, till their strength was all exhausted with the agony of their minds. Such prayers prevailed with God."

Scripture on the Spirit's Intercession

"In the same way, the Spirit helps us in our weakness. We do not know what we ought to pray for, but the Spirit himself intercedes for us through wordless groans." (Romans 8:26, NIV)

Jesus on Persistent Prayer

"Suppose one of you has a friend, and he goes to him at midnight and says, 'Friend, lend me three loaves of bread, because a friend of mine on a journey has come to me, and I have nothing to set before him.' Then the one inside answers, 'Don't bother me. The door is already locked, and my children are with me in bed. I can't get up and give you anything.' I tell you, though he will not get up and give him the bread because he is his friend, yet because of the man's boldness he will get up and give him as much as he needs." (Luke 11:5-8, NIV)

The Nature of Powerful Prayer

Prayer becomes powerful not through method but through surrender. When prayers cease to be negotiations and become worship. When the heart stops demanding and begins to yield.

3 THE PRICE OF FIRE: Death to Self and Total Surrender

Here is the hinge upon which everything turns. Prayer becomes powerful when the vessel is emptied of self and broken sufficiently for God to fill completely.

Key Quote

A willingness to deny self is the very spirit of Christ. It is the heart and soul of the Gospel. When you come back to God for pardon and salvation, come with all you have to lay at His feet. Give up all that you are and all that you have to God, and take Him at His word. This is the only way to find peace and rest for your souls.— Charles G. Finney

The Cost of Surrender

The cost is everything. Your agenda, ambition, pride, reputation, comfort, security—everything you think you cannot live without. God does not share His throne. He wants complete control, not second place.

Scripture on Self-Denial

"Whoever wants to be my disciple must deny themselves and take up their cross daily and follow me. For whoever wants to save their life will lose it, but whoever loses their life for me will save it." (Luke 9:23-24, NIV)

Scripture on Being Crucified with Christ

"I have been crucified with Christ and I no longer live, but Christ lives in me. The life I now live in the body, I live by faith in the Son of God, who loved me and gave himself for me." (Galatians 2:20, NIV)

Daily Crucifixion: The Way of Surrender

This is a daily crucifixion, constant death to self, absolute submission. The kingdom of heaven suffers violence, and the violent take it by force. As Finney explained, you must do violence to your own will, your own plans, your own ego until nothing remains but Christ.

Understanding Matthew 11:12

Matthew 11:12 – "The kingdom of heaven suffers violence."

The inner warfare of true Christianity is not violence toward others, but holy violence toward the self-life.

Key Points on Daily Crucifixion

  1. This is a daily crucifixion, constant death to self, absolute submission
  2. Christianity is not a one-time decision; it is a daily execution

Jesus in Luke 9:23 Teaches

  • Daily – not emotional moments, not a special meeting
  • Crucifixion – not adjustment, not improvement, but death
  • Submission – not negotiation, not partial obedience
  • Self must be put to death

What Matthew 11:12 Describes

Matthew 11:12 is describing spiritual aggression. This violence is against:

  • Comfort
  • Compromise
  • Flesh
  • Self-rule

It requires a yielded will. Human will naturally insists: "I choose, I decide, I control."

But God says (Luke 22:42): "Not my will, but Yours be done."

Scripture on Decrease

"He must become greater; I must become less." (John 3:30, NIV)

Finney's Own Struggle with Brokenness

God cannot trust His power to vessels still full of themselves. Finney often spoke of his own need for brokenness and surrender. He wrote about being in a state of great dissatisfaction with his own want of stability in faith and love. He needed frequently to hold days of fasting and prayer, and to spend much time overhauling his own religious life in order to retain that communion with God.

The Key: Brokenness Over Ability

God's principles:

  • God does not anoint abilities—He anoints brokenness
  • God does not use talent—He uses surrender
  • God does not seek strength—He seeks recognized weakness so His power can be manifested

Scripture on Power Perfected in Weakness

"But he said to me, 'My grace is sufficient for you, for my power is made perfect in weakness.' Therefore I will boast all the more gladly about my weaknesses, so that Christ's power may rest on me. That is why, for Christ's sake, I delight in weaknesses, in insults, in hardships, in persecutions, in difficulties. For when I am weak, then I am strong." (2 Corinthians 12:9-10, NIV)

4 THE UPPER ROOM PATTERN: Waiting Until Fire Falls

The apostles had walked with Jesus, witnessed miracles and resurrection. Yet they still lacked power—they had to wait in the upper room.

Scripture: The Apostles in Prayer

"They all joined together constantly in prayer, along with the women and Mary the mother of Jesus, and with his brothers." (Acts 1:14, NIV)

They remained in prayer, unity, and complete dependence until fire descended.

The Day of Pentecost

"When the day of Pentecost came, they were all together in one place. Suddenly a sound like the blowing of a violent wind came from heaven and filled the whole house where they were sitting. They saw what seemed to be tongues of fire that separated and came to rest on each of them. All of them were filled with the Holy Spirit." (Acts 2:1-4, NIV)

That fire did not illuminate just one room—it set an entire city ablaze. And from that city, the fire spread to other cities, other nations, until the entire world was reached.

The Apostolic Method

The apostles transformed the world not through eloquence or program, but through days of hidden prayer in the upper room until they became empty vessels the Holy Spirit could fill.

Peter's Pentecost Proclamation

Peter, who had denied Jesus three times, stood up on the day of Pentecost and proclaimed:

"Repent and be baptized, every one of you, in the name of Jesus Christ for the forgiveness of your sins. And you will receive the gift of the Holy Spirit. The promise is for you and your children and for all who are far off—for all whom the Lord our God will call." (Acts 2:38-39, NIV)

That day, about three thousand were added to their number. This is the power that comes from waiting in the secret place until fire falls.

The Compromise of Modern Christianity

Many want the mantle without the brokenness, want the authority without the humiliation, want to operate in power without going through the process. But God does not work that way.

Everyone wants the glory of Pentecost, but few want to wait ten days in the upper room. And because of this, we have churches full of activity but empty of power. We have services full of noise but empty of presence.

5 WHAT IS REVIVAL?: Understanding What We Seek

The Definition of Revival

"It's called revival because something that already had life has grown weak, asleep, or near death—and it needs to be brought back to life again."

You don't revive what was never alive. In the Bible, revival is not about converting sinners—that's evangelism. Revival is about awakening God's people.

Scripture on Revival

"Revive us again, that Your people may rejoice in You." (Psalm 85:6, NIV)

Israel already belonged to God—but their hearts drifted. The church already knows truth—but the fire fades. Revival is heaven breathing life back into a people who still believe but no longer burn.

That's why revival always starts inside, not outside.

Signs Revival Is Needed

It's called revival because:

  • Prayer is still happening—but without tears
  • Worship is still happening—but without awe
  • Preaching is still happening—but without conviction
  • People are still saved—but holiness is missing

What Revival Restores

Revival is God restoring:

  • First love
  • Fear of the Lord
  • Power of the Holy Spirit
  • Obedience, not just knowledge

Finney on Revival

Charles Finney said revival is not a miracle—it's the right use of the appointed means. In other words, when God's people repent, return, and realign, life rushes back in.

Revival is not new fire—it's old fire burning again. Not new truth—but truth obeyed. Not louder services—but cleaner hearts.— Charles G. Finney

That's why it's called revival—because God is reviving what belongs to Him.

6 THE MAN OF PRAYER: Father Nash

Finney knew a man named Father Nash who was most wonderful in prayer—one of the most earnest, devout, spiritually-minded, heavenly-minded men Finney ever saw. He gave himself up to almost constant prayer, literally praying himself to death.

He had a praying list of persons whom he made subjects of prayer every day, and sometimes many times a day. When Finney came to town to start a revival, a boarding house lady said, "Brother Finney, do you know a Father Nash? He and two other men have been at my boarding house for the last three days, but they haven't eaten a bite of food. I opened the door and peeped in at them because I could hear them groaning, and I saw them down on their faces."

When the public meetings started, Father Nash would not usually attend but kept praying in his closet for the convicting power of the Holy Spirit to fall on the crowd and melt their hearts. When opposition arose, Father Nash would pray all the harder.

This is the kind of hidden intercession that prepares the way for revival. Not public performance, but secret agony. Not platform presence, but closet persistence.

Scripture on Prayer for All Saints

"And pray in the Spirit on all occasions with all kinds of prayers and requests. With this in mind, be alert and always keep on praying for all the Lord's people." (Ephesians 6:18, NIV)

7 THE CALL TO DECISION: Will You Pay the Price?

This may be your hour—God has orchestrated circumstances, timing, everything—so you would hear this message now. Do not let it pass.

Finney's Direct Questions

Will you do it? Have you gone over with your sins and confessed them and gotten them all out of the way? Can you pray now? Will you join and offer prevailing prayer that the Spirit of God may come down here?— Charles G. Finney

Isaiah's Call to Seek God

"Seek the Lord while he may be found; call on him while he is near. Let the wicked forsake their ways and the unrighteous their thoughts. Let them turn to the Lord, and he will have mercy on them, and to our God, for he will freely pardon." (Isaiah 55:6-7, NIV)

The Four Stops

Stop playing at Christianity. Stop pretending everything is fine when you know it is not. Stop settling for crumbs when God offers His presence. Stop living on the periphery when God is calling you to the center of His will.

Stop trying to live for God and simply allow God to live through you.

Do not wait for better conditions—surrender is a present, immediate yielding. The fire spreads when one person burns completely for God. Be that one person who pays the price others refuse to pay.

Finney's Warning

Finney's Warning

"Unless you have the spirit of prayer, you can do nothing. You may preach, you may talk, you may distribute literature, you may do many things, but without prayer, without the Spirit of God, you will do nothing for the salvation of souls."

Scripture on Confidence Before God

"Let us then approach God's throne of grace with confidence, so that we may receive mercy and find grace to help us in our time of need." (Hebrews 4:16, NIV)

CONCLUSION: It Always Begins With One

It begins with one surrendered life—one person willing to enter the process of brokenness and total surrender.

Historical Examples

  • Noah alone preaching righteousness for 120 years
  • Abraham alone leaving everything to follow God's call
  • Elijah alone confronting Baal on Mount Carmel
  • John the Baptist alone crying in the wilderness
  • Jesus alone carrying the cross
  • The apostles few in number waiting in the upper room

It always begins with one.

The Two Paths

Without total surrender, you can preach, sing, serve—but produce no eternal fruit. With total surrender, dormant resurrection power awakens within you. What begins as one burning life spreads to families, communities, churches, and cities.

The Fire of Revival

The fire of revival does not fall on churches with the best programs or most talented leaders. It falls on churches whose people have learned the secret of total surrender and prevailing prayer.

Scripture on Working Out Your Salvation

"Continue to work out your salvation with fear and trembling, for it is God who works in you to will and to act in order to fulfill his good purpose." (Philippians 2:12-13, NIV)

The Requirements for the Spirit of Prayer

If you would have this spirit of prayer, you must:

  • Give yourselves to prayer
  • Make time for prayer
  • Pray in your families
  • Pray in secret
  • Pray much
  • Pray earnestly
  • Pray in faith
  • Pray with perseverance

Final Prayer

May God help us to be a praying people, and to seek His face until He comes and rains righteousness upon us.

Altar Call Prayer

"Here I am—take everything—break me, empty me, ignite in me a fire that will never go out."

• • •

Final Truth

What differentiates ordinary Christianity from life that sets multitudes ablaze is not gift, talent, education, or opportunity. It is total surrender—a life hidden with Christ in God, completely yielded to His will and His glory.
